We greatly value books, especially timeless classics.

Musashi by Eiji Yoshikawa

A sweeping historical novel that fictionalizes the life of Miyamoto Musashi, Japan’s legendary swordsman. It follows his transformation from a reckless warrior into a disciplined master of both swordsmanship and philosophy, emphasizing themes of self-mastery, honor, and the pursuit of enlightenment.

1984 by George Orwell

A dystopian classic set in a totalitarian society ruled by Big Brother. Winston Smith, a low-ranking Party member, struggles against oppressive surveillance, propaganda, and thought control, ultimately exploring the dangers of authoritarianism and the fragility of truth and freedom.

The Art of War by Sun Tzu

An ancient Chinese military treatise composed of 13 chapters, each detailing strategies and tactics for warfare. Beyond the battlefield, its principles—such as deception, adaptability, and knowing both yourself and your enemy—have influenced leadership, business, and competitive strategy worldwide.

White Nights by Fyodor Dostoevsky

A poignant short story set in St. Petersburg, centering on a lonely dreamer who meets a young woman during four summer nights. Their fleeting connection explores themes of isolation, longing, and the bittersweet nature of unfulfilled love, capturing Dostoevsky’s deep psychological insight.
